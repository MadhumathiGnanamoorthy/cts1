Meningioma

What is Meningioma?

Meningioma is a type of tumor that arises from the meninges, the membranes that surround the brain and spinal cord. While most meningiomas are benign, meaning they are not cancerous, some can be atypical or malignant, growing more aggressively.

Meningiomas account for about 30% of all primary brain tumors and are more common in women than men. They can occur at any age but are most often diagnosed in older adults.

Types of Meningioma

  • Benign Meningioma: The most common type, these tumors are slow-growing and less likely to spread.
  • Atypical Meningioma: These tumors grow more quickly and have a higher risk of recurrence after treatment.
  • Anaplastic (Malignant) Meningioma: The rarest type, these tumors are aggressive, grow quickly, and can spread to other parts of the brain or body.

Symptoms

The symptoms of meningioma depend on the tumor's size and location. Common symptoms include:

  • Headaches, which can be severe and worsen over time.
  • Seizures, particularly in those who have never had seizures before.
  • Vision problems, such as double vision or partial vision loss.
  • Hearing loss or ringing in the ears.
  • Memory loss or changes in cognitive function.
  • Weakness in the limbs or difficulty walking.
  • Personality changes or mood swings.
